Types of projects

All projects will fall into these four categories.

  • Easy to launch microservices
  • Data sets/machine learning/visualisations
  • Big basic core services that allow for smaller experiments
  • Integrations and 3rd party extensions

Why are we starting this?

We’ve worked in the Politics & NGO sector in Australia and the US. We’ve worked in agencies, political parties, big and small NGOs. Technology is not being done well in these spaces and neither is utilising the many skilled volunteers looking for quality opportunities to do something meaningful.

Progressive movements need better tools and better clarity on how to manage technology within their organisations. By creating an open source community we hope to make it easy to access quality tools and talent to ensure organisations are not held back by bad decisions and are able to utilise good technology to maximise their impact on progressive issues.

We have seen great examples of one off projects that have rallied volunteers and have made a huge impact amongst progressive organisations. We want to take these examples and try create a community that can regularly discuss and develop tools in a sustainable way. Traditional community organising is great at mobilising and supporting a community of volunteers. We want to combine that with our experience building digital tools to create a highly effective community of digital activists building tools for good.

Measure Outcomes

We measure the success of our principals by quantifying these outcomes

  • More quality OSS being used by organisations
  • Strong community of regular volunteers working on great projects
  • Organisations are able to afford/do more custom experiments that suite their needs because their stack is build on quality flexaible OSS
  • More OSS that has been strongly informed by direct organisation need thus having strong adoption and use amongst organisations
  • More organisations able to build good inhouse tech teams or effectively utilise good freelancers because of reduced cost of work involved via use of quality OSS
